1. Purpose and Core Decision Principle
This document defines the calculations and decision rules IFM uses after source data has been mapped into standard IFM domains. Source system should affect validation and confidence, not the core formula.
Locked Hard Rules
IFM is source-agnostic. QuickBooks and Keystroke are example/test sources, not required systems.
IFM maps source data into standard IFM data domains before calculations run.
IFM does not create, send, modify, cancel, pay, borrow, or factor automatically in V1.5.
Funding approval is separate from purchase approval and separate from official PO action.
Buyer action in Keystroke or another purchasing system happens outside IFM in V1.5.
LOC and factoring are supplemental funding, not operating cash.
Vendor deals, discounts, free freight, and extended terms are not automatically good.
RSE identifies replenishment need and inventory classification; IFM allocates funding priority.
Blank or missing data must not be treated as zero unless confirmed none exist.
Any file intended for Keystroke upload or re-import must be tab-delimited and end in .txt.
CSV/XLSX may be used for review, mapping, QA, internal analysis, or customer review, but not as Keystroke import-ready files.
Customer-facing output must protect sensitive internal, financing, AR, AP, and reviewer detail unless approved for display.

6. Supplemental Funding Calculation
Supplemental Funding Capacity = Approved Inventory LOC Capacity + Approved Net Factoring Proceeds + Other Approved Short-Term Funding. Total Potential Inventory Funding = Core Available Inventory Funding + Supplemental Funding Capacity.
Supplemental source
Calculation / rule
Line of Credit
Available LOC = LOC limit - current balance - borrowing-base limitation. Approved inventory LOC = min(available LOC, management-approved inventory draw limit). LOC is not cash and requires approval when approval_required = yes.
Factoring
Net proceeds = eligible AR x advance rate - factoring fee - reserve holdback. Factored AR cannot also be counted as expected collection.
Other funding
Must be management-approved, separately labeled, and risk-noted.
7. No Double-Counting Rule
AR cannot be counted as both expected collection and factored proceeds.
LOC availability cannot be counted if already drawn into cash unless clearly classified.
Open PO exposure should not also be counted as AP unless received/billed status supports AP treatment.
Factoring proceeds must be net of fees and holdbacks.
Replacement uploads and superseded records should not feed active calculations unless restored by reviewer.

10. Keystroke or Other Reorder Logic Treatment
Suggested order/reorder data is evidence and can create purchase candidates, but it is not IFM approval. This rule applies to Keystroke and to equivalent ERP/POS reorder reports. Keystroke import-ready outputs, if generated, must be tab-delimited .txt.
